This is a want-to-read list, not a claim of mastery. I keep it public because it shows the intellectual direction I want to build toward.

## Intelligence and Models

- **Godel, Escher, Bach**, Douglas Hofstadter. For thinking about recursion, analogy, and cognition.
- **The Society of Mind**, Marvin Minsky. For an older but still productive model of intelligence as many cooperating processes.
- **Probability Theory: The Logic of Science**, E. T. Jaynes. For Bayesian reasoning as disciplined inference.
- **The Beginning of Infinity**, David Deutsch. For explanations, progress, and the reach of scientific knowledge.

## Systems and Institutions

- **Thinking in Systems**, Donella Meadows. For understanding feedback, leverage points, and system behavior.
- **Seeing Like a State**, James C. Scott. For how institutions simplify reality and what breaks when they do.
- **The Structure of Scientific Revolutions**, Thomas Kuhn. For how research programs change over time.
- **The Idea Factory**, Jon Gertner. For how Bell Labs turned research culture into durable technical output.

## Engineering and Execution

- **Skunk Works**, Ben Rich. For high-agency engineering under constraint.
- **The Making of the Atomic Bomb**, Richard Rhodes. For the relationship between scientific discovery, institutions, and technical execution.
- **The Dream Machine**, M. Mitchell Waldrop. For computing history and the people who shaped interactive systems.
- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications**, Martin Kleppmann. For distributed systems fundamentals and engineering taste.
