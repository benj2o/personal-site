The IEEE Conference on Secure and Trustworthy Machine Learning (SaTML) is an annual forum focused on the offensive and defensive dimensions of AI/ML security. The most recent Munich edition took place March 23 to 25, 2026, hosted physically by the Technical University of Munich (TUM).

{{< reflection-figure src="images/ieee-satml-2026.png" caption="Presenting BinaryShield at SaTML, hosted at the Technical University of Munich." >}}

This was my first IEEE conference. I presented BinaryShield, work on cross-service threat intelligence in LLM services using privacy-preserving fingerprints, at the poster session.

SaTML was a useful mix of attack research and defensive tooling. Most conversations were practical: how threats propagate across API boundaries, what can be shared without leaking user data, and where detection actually holds up at scale.
