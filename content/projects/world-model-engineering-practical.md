My professors have always been incredibly adamant that AI as we know it, meaning LLMs, is unintelligent. Whether that even matters is another question, but I think they are directionally right. My work at TUM focuses on understanding and advancing world models and teach machines to become intelligent.

How I even got here:
After five persistent emails, I got to work on the question directly. The underlying hypothesis is that a useful world model needs more than fluent prediction. It needs structure about space, time, causality, and what remains stable when the camera moves.
